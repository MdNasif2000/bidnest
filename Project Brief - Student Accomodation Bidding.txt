Project Brief
Company Name: BidNest - Student Accommodation Renting & Bidding Platform 
- "Where Every Bid Counts"
Online Presence: To be developed (Web Based)

Background

Finding suitable accommodation is one of the biggest challenges faced by students in the UK. While there are websites for general rental listings, there is currently no dedicated platform where students can bid for accommodations, post their requirements, or negotiate directly with landlords in a secure and transparent environment.
BidNest aims to address these gaps by providing a web-based marketplace specifically designed for student accommodation. The platform will integrate bidding, secure payments, direct communication, and digital documentation, ensuring that both students and landlords can manage the entire accommodation process from start to finish in one place.

Key features include:
> Bidding on preferred properties.
> Posting detailed accommodation requirements.
> Direct messaging between students and landlords.
> Secure payment using an escrow system.
> Online contract creation, signing, and document verification for legal transparency.
> Automated notifications for contract expiry or renewal.
BidNest will initially be developed as a web platform, with a potential mobile version in future phases.

Problem Statement
Students often face difficulties such as:
> Limited visibility of suitable accommodations.
> Difficulty finding affordable, student-friendly housing close to their university.
> Lack of transparency in pricing and property availability.
> No bidding or negotiation system in existing UK platforms.
> Unclear or insecure payment methods.
> Manual, time-consuming document and agreement handling.

Landlords face challenges including:
> Difficulty reaching verified student tenants.
> Managing multiple offers and inquiries efficiently.
> Lack of secure and timely payment options.
> Manual handling of tenancy agreements and identity verification.

There is a clear need for a dedicated, student-oriented online platform that streamlines every stage of the accommodation process — from search and negotiation to contract signing and payment — while ensuring security, convenience, and transparency for both parties.

Work Related Project Process
Typically, a work-related project would involve going through the software development life cycle as indicated below:
1. Project Management
Apply a project management methodology in planning, development, management and execution of the project. Deliverables include, but are not limited to, documents such as project plan, cost-benefit analysis, Gantt chart, Work Breakdown Structure, Risk Assessment.

2. Software Engineering
The following stages of the software development lifecycle will generally be required to develop the system, although this could be altered depending on the software methodology being used.
* Feasibility Study – Technical feasibility and schedule feasibility will be evaluated at this stage in order to be certain about what the project entails.
* Requirements gathering – This will involve using techniques such as interviews or questionnaires or focus groups as well as user stories to understand the problem. 
* Requirements Analysis/Specification – This involves analysing the requirements from a technical point of view and specifying the functional and non-functional requirements.
* Design – This stage will involve creating high-level and low-level representations of the system in the form of diagrams (e.g. UML) showing the architecture and design of the system.
* Implementation (Coding) – This stage will involve using a programming language to develop the system.
* Testing – This stage will involve carrying out various tests on the system such as unit testing, User acceptance testing, black-box testing etc. to ensure that the system is fit for use.
* Configuration and Deployment – This will involve deployment of the system for the client to use.
